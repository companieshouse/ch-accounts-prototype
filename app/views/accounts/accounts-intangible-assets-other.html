Intangible assets: Other

Cost

At 1 April 2015

The cost or valuation of all goodwill assets at the start of the financial period.

Additions

The cost of all goodwill assets acquired during the financial period.

Disposals

The cost or valuation (at the date of disposal) of all goodwill assets that were disposed of (for example, sold) during the financial period.

Revaluations

The valuation figure of all goodwill assets revalued during the financial period. If the revaluation results in a reduction in the value of the asset, then enter a negative figure using a minus sign.

Transfers

The cost or valuation of all goodwill assets that have been transferred during the financial period. If the asset is being transferred out, then enter a negative figure using a minus sign.

At 31 March 2016

The total cost or valuation of all goodwill assets at the end of the financial period.

This is calculated by adding together any additions, revaluations and transfers to the cost or valuation of the asset(s) at the start of the financial period, and then subtracting any disposals.

Amortisation

At 1 April 2015

The cumulative amount of amortisation of all goodwill assets at the start of the financial period.

Amortisation is the reduction in value of an intangible (non-physical) asset over its useful economic lifetime. It’s similar to the depreciation value of a tangible (physical) asset..

On disposals

The amortisation of all goodwill assets for the financial period.

Amortisation is the reduction in value of an intangible (non-physical) asset over its useful economic lifetime. It’s similar to the depreciation value of a tangible (physical) asset.

Other adjustments

Any other amortisation adjustment on all goodwill assets.

Amortisation is the reduction in value of an intangible (non-physical) asset over its useful economic lifetime. It’s similar to the depreciation value of a tangible (physical) asset.

At 31 March 2016

The total amortisation value of all goodwill assets at the end of the financial period.

This is calculated by adding together any goodwill assets provided during the period, other adjustments and the amortisation value at the start of the financial period, and then subtracting any disposals.

Net book value

At 31 March 2015

The total amortisation value of all goodwill assets at the end of the financial period.

This is calculated by adding together any goodwill assets provided during the period, other adjustments and the amortisation value at the start of the financial period, and then subtracting any disposals.

At 31 March 2016

The net book value of all goodwill assets at the end of the previous financial period (balance sheet date).

This will be the total cost or valuation of all goodwill assets at the end of the previous financial period, minus the total amortisation value of all goodwill assets at the end of the previous financial period.
