4 Balance sheet notes Revaluation reserve

Use this note to provide information about the revaluation reserve value from your balance sheet.

 

£

At 1 April 2016

The total amount of revaluation reserve (surplus or deficit) at the start of the financial period.

This amount is usually already given in the box, as it is the revaluation reserve figure you entered in your balance sheet for the previous year.

If the revaluation results in a deficit (reduction in value) of the asset, then you’ll see a negative value with a minus sign.

Surplus or deficit arising from revaluation

The cumulative surplus or deficit (increase or reduction) in the value of your company's tangible (physical) and intangible (non-physical) assets after a revaluation. For example, an increase in the value of land after a revaluation.

If the revaluation results in a deficit (reduction in value) of the asset, then enter a negative value using a minus sign.

At 31 March 2017

The total amount of revaluation reserve at the end of the accounting period.

This is calculated by adding together the revaluation reserve at the start of the accounting period and the surplus arising from revaluations (or subtracting the deficit arising from revaluations).
