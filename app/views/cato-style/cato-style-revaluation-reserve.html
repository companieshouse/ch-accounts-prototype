These are the figures you entered on the balance sheet:

Revaluation reserve
At 1 April 2015

The total amount of revaluation reserve (surplus or deficit) at the start of the financial period.

The figure is typically pre-populated with the revaluation reserve figure entered in your balance sheet for the previous year.

If the revaluation results in a deficit (reduction in value) of the asset, then a negative value will be entered using a minus sign.

Surplus or deficit arising from revaluation

The cumulative surplus or deficit (increase or reduction) in the value of your company's tangible (physical) and intangible (non-physical) assets following a revaluation. For example, an increase in the value of land following a revaluation.

If the revaluation results in a deficit (reduction in value) of the asset, then enter a negative value using a minus sign.

At 31 March 2016

The total amount of revaluation reserve at the end of the accounting period.

This is calculated by adding together the revaluation reserve at the start of the accounting period and the surplus arising from revaluations (or subtracting the deficit arising from revaluations).
