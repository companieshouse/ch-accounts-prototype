You can no longer file abbreviated accounts

Changes in Company Law have withdrawn the option for filing abbreviated accounts for accounting periods starting on or after 1st January 2016.

Instead, choose the type of accounts that satisfies the business requirements for this company and the amount of information which will be publicly available.

Try the new abridged accounts filing

We’re launching a new private beta service which would allow you to file abridged accounts.

More information

You can try the new service if:

  • your accounting period starts on or after 1 January 2016
  • your balance sheet doesn’t include 'intangible assets', 'fixed asset investments' or 'revaluation reserve'
  • you just need to supply an 'accounting policies' note (and, if needed, a 'tangible assets' note and/or a 'creditors - amounts falling due within one year’ note)
  • you don't need to change the date the accounts are made up to

At least 2 of these must also apply to the company:

  • turnover is no more than £10.2 million
  • balance sheet total is no more than £5.1 million
  • average number of employees is no more than 50
