Bank loans and overdrafts

The total value of bank loans and overdrafts your company owes and expects to pay within one year from the end of the period.

Amounts due under finance leases and hire purchase contracts

The total of capital amounts (original values) your company owes for finance leases and hire purchase contracts and expects to pay within one year from the end of the period.

Trade creditors

The total your company owes to trade creditors and expects to pay within one year from the end of the period.

A trade creditor is someone that your company owes for debts incurred during trading operations, such as buying goods or materials from suppliers for resale.

Taxation and social security

The total of all tax and social security your company is due to pay within one year from the end of the period.

This is normally the amounts due for:

  • corporation tax
  • PAYE
  • National Insurance
  • VAT
Accruals and deferred income

The total accruals and deferred income your company expects to pay within one year from the end of the period.

Accruals: Expenses that are not yet paid for, for example, electricity already used but to be paid in the future.

Deferred income: The amount you were paid for goods and services that you hadn't provided at the end of the period, for example, a client deposit paid before work starts.

Other creditors

Any other amounts that your company owes and expects to pay within one year from the end of the period. Exclude anything already included in this note.

Total

The total value of all creditors amounts falling due within one year.

This is calculated by adding together:

  • bank loans and overdrafts
  • amounts due under finance leases and hire purchase contracts
  • trade creditors
  • taxation and social security
  • accruals and deferred income
  • other creditors
Add secured creditors and additional information? (optional)
