5 Balance sheet notes Revaluation reserve

Use this note to provide information about the revaluation reserve value from your balance sheet.

 

£

At 1 April 2017

The total amount of revaluation reserve (surplus or deficit) at the start of the financial period.

This amount is usually already given in the box, as it is the revaluation reserve figure you entered in your balance sheet for the previous year.

If the revaluation results in a deficit (reduction in value) of the asset, then you’ll see a negative value with a minus sign.

Surplus or deficit after revaluation

The total increase or reduction in the value of all tangible (physical) and intangible (non-physical) assets that were revalued in the period.

If assets reduced in value after a revaluation, enter a negative value using a minus sign. For example, if your company's machinery was valued at £10,000 less than the previous period, enter -10000.

Balance at 31 March 2017

The total revaluation reserve at the end of the period. This is calculated by adding together the revaluation reserve at the start of the period and any surplus arising from revaluations (or subtracting any deficit arising from revaluations).

This should match the revaluation reserve figure you entered in the balance sheet for the period you're filing for.
